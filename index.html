    • Give it to her and Emma will give you a Dragon's Blood Droplet to cure the afflicted npcs. You can buy more from vendors. Only use them when you want to continue the npcs storylines.
    • Emma will give you Ashina Sake

    New Playthroughs

    • Even though you can obtain the four endings individually (or by save-scumming) you need to do do at least one "new game plus" to have all the memory of the bosses at the same time
      • Immortal Severance / Purification / Return ending to obtain Memory: Saint Isshin
      • Shura ending to obtain Memory: Isshin Ashina
    • You also need to go for the Immortal Severance / Purification / Return ending at least two times to obtain all the Lapis Lazuli that you need to obtain all the upgrades for the prosthetic arm. Also, the best place to farm experience is after going for the non-shura endings.
      • On your first playthrough you should have obtained 6 Lapis lazuli
      • You need 4 more to have all the upgrades, but in each game cycle you can obtain another 6.
        • You can kill the Shichimen warrior in the Fountainhead Palace to obtain 1x Lapis Lazuli
        • You can defeat the Demon of Hatred to obtain 2x Lapis Lazuli
        • You can buy 2x Lapis Lazuli out of Pot Noble Koremori
        • You can obtain 1x Lapis Lazuli by ending the Pot Noble Quests

    Treasure Carp Scale

    • Hirata Estate:
      • 2x found from a Treasure Carp swimming to the right side of the bridge, near the Pot Noble Harunaga.
      • 2x found from a Treasure Carp underwater in the same lake. Can be both killed from land with a changed Spear.
      • 1x found on the riverbank to the left of the bridge before crossing.
      • 2x found from Treasure Carp past Bamboo Thicket Sculptor's Idol in the lake underneath the bridge with the large enemy. Diving is required for one of them.
    • Ashina Castle:
      • 1x found from a Treasure Carp in the castle moat. Diving is required (only spawns once Ashina Castle is attacked)
      • 1x found as a pickup in the castle moat beneath the idol. Diving is required (only spawns once Ashina Castle is attacked)
    • Senpou Temple, Mt. Kongo:
      • 2x found from Treasure Carps in the lake near the Temple Grounds Sculptor's Idol.
    • Sunken Valley:
      • 2x found from Treasure Carps found in a lake near the Riven Cave Sculptor's Idol
      • 1x found from a pickup in the lake near the Riven Cave Sculptor's Idol. Diving is required.
    • Ashina Depths:
      • 2x sold by Exiled Memorial Mob for 500 sen each.
      • 1x found below the Mibu Village idol guarded by a Ministry Shinobi.
      • 1x found from a Treasure Carp found in Mibu Village river near the bridge. Diving may be needed.
      • 5x found by giving the Mibu Village priest Water of the Palace, then reloading the area to find him transformed into an enemy which will drop the scales upon being killed.
    • Fountainhead Palace:
      • 3x found in a room accessed by a cracked floor and underwater route also contains Water of the Palace.
      • 7x found from Treasure Carps in the area's main body of water. Diving is required
      • 3x found in a small sunken house beneath the area's main body of water. Diving is required.
      • 2x found by the giant carp skeleton with worms on it at the bottom of the body of water. Diving is required.
      • 4x found by killing the Feeding Ground Attendant, after feeding the Giant Comiscd Carp a 'Truly Precious Bait' on first feeding.
      • 1x found by feeding the Giant Comiscd Carp 'Precious Bait', then talking to misc next to the bell (first feeding).
      • 3x found by feeding the Giant Comiscd Carp 'Precious Bait', then talking to misc next to the bell (second feeding).
      • If you did not feed the Giant comiscd carp and receive the 3 scales, it can be found on the body of the misc at the feeding grounds once the daughter is by his corpse.

    FAQ

    The foundation of this guide was based on Zachary Kjellberg's Dark Soul 3 Cheat Sheet and Stephen McNabb's Dark Soul 2 Cheat Sheet source code. I only copy & paste and learn along the way. Each checkbox is based on Sekiro's Game Progress Route Wiki. I completed the guide in a effort to include all key items, sakes, minibosses etc, in a unified way

    I have feedback, how can I contribute?

    I have a post on reddit. You can send me a DM or answer that post to send me any comment or criticism about the Cheat Sheet.

    Is this a guide?

    Not exactly. This is a set of checklists and information that you can use while playing Sekiro. This is useful to to make sure you don't miss an important key item or boss. I think this is cool to use in a second playthrough, once you don't mind so much about spoilers and want to do everything

    Do I need to follow the playthrough in this exact order?

    Sekiro has multiple ways of progressing. I have follow the Sekiro's Game Progress Route Wiki. Some minibosses can be done in a different order, before or after that some key bosses.

    Can I use this for multiple characters?

    Yes, use the profile selector and buttons in the options tab at the top of the page to setup multiple profiles.

    How does the checklist status get saved?

    The checklist is saved to your browser's local storage. Be careful when clearing your browser's cache as it will also destroy your saved progress.
